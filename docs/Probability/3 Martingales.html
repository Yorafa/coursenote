Martingales

Recall the Martingales we learn in STA 347. In this section, we will review the definition of a martingale and some of its properties.

Introduction and Definition​

Martingales are a model for stochastic processes which “stay the same on average” and a model of fair game.

That is, a sequence XnX_nXn​ with finite expectation is a martingale if E[Xn+1∣X0,X1,…,Xn]=XnE[X_{n+1} | X_0, X_1, \ldots, X_n] = X_nE[Xn+1​∣X0​,X1​,…,Xn​]=Xn​ for all n≥0n \geq 0n≥0 or equivalently, ∑j∈Sjpij=i,∀i∈S\sum_{j\in S} jp_{ij} = i, \forall i \in S∑j∈S​jpij​=i,∀i∈S.
