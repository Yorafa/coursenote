Markov Chain Monte Carlo

Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) is a methodology that use Markov sequences to efficiently model otherwise intractable distributions. That is, Given a probability distribution π\piπ, the goal of MCMC is to simulate a random variable XXX whose distribution is π\piπ. Such distribution may continuous or discrete even though we assume it's discrete.

Metropolis-Hastings Algorithm​

The most common MCMC method is Metropolis-Hastings algorithm. It produces an irreducible, aperiodic Markov chain with stationary distribution π\piπ. Specifically:

Let π\piπ be a discrete probability distribution. Let PPP be a transition matrix for any irreducible Markov chain with the same state space as π\piπ. PPP used as a proposal chain which generates elements of a sequence that the algorithm decides whether or not to accept (assumed knows how to sample from PPP). The algorithm constructs a reversible Markov chain X0,X1,…X_0, X_1, \ldotsX0​,X1​,… whose stationary distribution is π\piπ

Otherwise, accept jjj with probability α(i,j)\alpha(i,j)α(i,j). If jjj is not aaccepted, then we keep iii as the next state of the chain. (i.e. Xn+1={jif U≤α(i,j)iif U>α(i,j)X_{n+1} = \begin{cases} j & \text{if } U \le \alpha(i,j) \\ i & \text{if } U > \alpha(i,j) \end{cases}Xn+1​={ji​if U≤α(i,j)if U>α(i,j)​)

  • uniform π\piπ lead α(i,j)=pjipij\alpha(i,j) = \frac{p_{ji}}{p_{ij}}α(i,j)=pij​pji​​

  • PPP is ergodic, then resulting chain is ergodic
  • long run may lead bias and burn-in (i.e. the discarding of the initial iterations)
