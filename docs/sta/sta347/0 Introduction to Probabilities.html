Let Ω\OmegaΩ be some abstract space, Let F∈℘(Ω),\mathcal{F}\in \wp(\Omega),F∈℘(Ω), F\mathcal{F}F is a σ−\sigma-σ−algebra if satisfies the following properties:

  1. A∈Σ  ⟹  Ac∈ΣA\in \Sigma \implies A^c \in \SigmaA∈Σ⟹Ac∈Σ
  2. Closed under the formation of complements, countable unions and countable intersections.
  3. ∅∈Σ∧Ω∈Σ\empty\in \Sigma \land \Omega\in \Sigma∅∈Σ∧Ω∈Σ

Usually, we have some σ−\sigma-σ−algebra property:

  • The intersection of σ\sigmaσ-algebra is still σ\sigmaσ-algebra, but union is not

Let F⊆℘(X)\mathcal{F}\subseteq \wp(\mathbb{X})F⊆℘(X) be σ\sigmaσ-algebra and μ\muμ be a measure on F\mathcal{F}F.

  • (X,F)(\mathbb{X}, \mathcal{F})(X,F) is a measurable space
