Group Isomorphisms

Isomorphism: ∀a,b∈G,φ:G→G′,φ(ab)=φ(a)φ(b)  ⟹  φ\forall a,b \in G, \varphi: G\to G', \varphi(ab) = \varphi(a)\varphi(b) \implies \varphi∀a,b∈G,φ:G→G′,φ(ab)=φ(a)φ(b)⟹φ is bijective, then φ\varphiφ is a isomorphism.

  • GGG is isomorphic to G′G'G′ note as G≅G′G\cong G'G≅G′

  • Any group is isomorphic to itself (reflexive)

  • G≅G′  ⟹  G′≅GG\cong G' \implies G' \cong GG≅G′⟹G′≅G (symmetric)

  • G≅H,H≅F  ⟹  G≅FG\cong H, H\cong F \implies G \cong FG≅H,H≅F⟹G≅F (transitive)

  • a bijective function φ:G→G\varphi : G \to Gφ:G→G is isomorphism   ⟹  φ\implies \varphi⟹φ is an automorphism of GGG

    • the set of all automorphisms of GGG denote as Aut(G)Aut(G)Aut(G)

  • any isomorphism is a homomorphism (but not vice versa)

  • G≅ZnG\cong \Z^nG≅Zn, we define its rank rk(G)=nrk(G) = nrk(G)=n

  1. Define φ:G→G′\varphi: G\to G'φ:G→G′, ∀a,b∈G,φ(ab)=φ(a)φ(b)\forall a,b\in G, \varphi(ab) = \varphi(a)\varphi(b)∀a,b∈G,φ(ab)=φ(a)φ(b)

Isomorphism Property​

let φ:G→G‾\varphi: G\to \overline{G}φ:G→G be isomorphism :

  • ∀g∈G,ord(g)=ord(φ(g))\forall g\in G, ord(g) = ord(\varphi(g))∀g∈G,ord(g)=ord(φ(g))
  • ϕ\phiϕ carries the identity of GGG to the identity of G‾\overline{G}G
  • GGG is cyclic   ⟺  \iff⟺ G‾\overline{G}G is cyclic (i.e. G=⟨g⟩  ⟺  G‾=⟨φ(g)⟩G = \langle g\rangle \iff \overline{G} = \langle \varphi(g)\rangleG=⟨g⟩⟺G=⟨φ(g)⟩)

Automorphism​

An isomorphism φ:G→G\varphi: G\to Gφ:G→G is called an automorphism of GGG.

Let GGG be a group, a∈Ga \in Ga∈G. The function ϕa,s.t.,∀x∈G,ϕa(x)=axa−1\phi_a, s.t., \forall x\in G, \phi_a(x) = axa^{-1}ϕa​,s.t.,∀x∈G,ϕa​(x)=axa−1 is called the inner automorphism of GGG induced by aaa.

The set of automorphisms of GGG is denoted by Aut(G)Aut(G)Aut(G) and the set of inner automorphisms of GGG is denoted by Inn(G)Inn(G)Inn(G). They are both groups under operation of composition.
