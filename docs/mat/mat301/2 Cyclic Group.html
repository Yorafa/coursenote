Cyclic Group

We define G={an:n∈Z}=⟨a⟩G = \{a^n:n\in\Z\} = \langle a \rangleG={an:n∈Z}=⟨a⟩ is a Cyclic Group.

  • ∀g∈G,⟨g⟩\forall g\in G, \langle g \rangle∀g∈G,⟨g⟩ is a subgroup of GGG (cyclic subgroup). ggg is the genrator here.

  • cyclic group is abelian

  • H≤GH\le GH≤G is a subgroup of GGG, g∈Gg\in Gg∈G, g∈H  ⟺  ⟨g⟩⊆Hg\in H \iff \langle g\rangle \subseteq Hg∈H⟺⟨g⟩⊆H

  • ∣a∣=∣⟨a⟩∣|a| = |\langle a\rangle|∣a∣=∣⟨a⟩∣

  • subgroups of cyclic groups are cyclic, ∣⟨a⟩∣=n  ⟹  |\langle a\rangle| = n \implies∣⟨a⟩∣=n⟹ the order of any subgroup of ⟨a⟩\langle a \rangle⟨a⟩ is a divisor of nnn; ∀k∣n,k>0\forall k|n, k > 0∀k∣n,k>0, the group ⟨a⟩\langle a\rangle ⟨a⟩ has exactly one subgroup of order kkk

  • Let H≤GH \le GH≤G be a subgroup, and let g∈Gg\in Gg∈G, then g∈Hg\in Hg∈H is equivalent to ⟨g⟩⊆H\langle g \rangle \sube H⟨g⟩⊆H

  • let k∈Z+,k∣n  ⟹  ϕ(d)=k\in \Z^+, k|n \implies \phi(d) =k∈Z+,k∣n⟹ϕ(d)= number of elements (with order ddd) in a cyclic group of order nnn

    • ∣⟨a⟩∣≠∞,|\langle a\rangle| \ne \infty,∣⟨a⟩∣=∞, the number of elements of order ddd is a multiple of ϕ(d)\phi(d)ϕ(d)

  • order:

    • The smallest positive integer n,gn=e  ⟹  ∣g∣=nn, g^n = e \implies |g| = nn,gn=e⟹∣g∣=n or infinite order (order of element of group)
    • ∣g∣=n  ⟹  gk⋅gl=gk+l(mod n)|g| = n \implies g^k\cdot g^l = g^{k+l(mod \ n)}∣g∣=n⟹gk⋅gl=gk+l(mod n)
    • ak=e=an  ⟹  agcd(k,n)=ea^k = e = a^n\implies a^{gcd(k,n)}= eak=e=an⟹agcd(k,n)=e or ∣a∣=gcd{n∈N:an=e}|a| = gcd\{n\in\N : a^n = e\}∣a∣=gcd{n∈N:an=e}

Cyclic Group Theorem​

Theorem 1​

∣⟨a⟩∣=∞  ⟹  (ai=aj  ⟺  i=j)|\langle a\rangle| = \infty \implies (a^i = a^j \iff i = j)∣⟨a⟩∣=∞⟹(ai=aj⟺i=j)

∣⟨a⟩∣≠∞  ⟹  (ai=aj  ⟺  n∣(i−j))|\langle a\rangle| \ne \infty \implies (a^i = a^j \iff n | (i-j))∣⟨a⟩∣=∞⟹(ai=aj⟺n∣(i−j))

Theorem 2​

∣a∣=n  ⟹  ⟨ak⟩=⟨agcd(n,k)⟩|a| = n \implies \langle a^k \rangle = \langle a^{gcd(n,k)}\rangle∣a∣=n⟹⟨ak⟩=⟨agcd(n,k)⟩ where k∈Z+k \in \Z^+k∈Z+

  • ∣a∣=n|a| = n∣a∣=n.
