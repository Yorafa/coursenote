Permutation Group

Permutation of a set is a bijection function A→AA\to AA→A.

  • Every permutation is the product of swaps, even permutation is an even number of swaps

Permutation Group of a set AAA: a set of permutations of AAA forms a group with respect to the composition.

  • Every permutation of a finite set is a product of disjoint cycles or a cycle.
  • The order of a permutation of a finite set written in disjoint cycle form is the lcm of the lengths of the cycles.
  • Every permutation in Sn,n>1S_n, n > 1Sn​,n>1 is a product of 2-cycles

Cycle​

A cycle α=(a1a2⋯ak)\alpha = (a_1a_2\cdots a_k)α=(a1​a2​⋯ak​) is a permutation satisfied:

  • {a1,⋯ ,ak}\{a_1,\cdots, a_k\}{a1​,⋯,ak​} is the support of cycle
  • α(ak)=a1,k\alpha(a^k) = a_1, kα(ak)=a1​,k is the length of α\alphaα
  • α(ai)=ai+1,1≤i<k\alpha(a_i) = a_{i+1}, 1 \le i < kα(ai​)=ai+1​,1≤i<k
  • α(b)=b,b∉{a1,⋯ ,ak}\alpha(b) = b, b\notin \{a_1,\cdots, a_k\}α(b)=b,b∈/{a1​,⋯,ak​}
  • 2-cycles (i,j)(i,j)(i,j) called transposition
  • We say α\alphaα is even if α\alphaα is a product of even number of 2-cycles, odd otherwise
